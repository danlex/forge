You fixed it! The bug was a case mismatch between your vowel set and your input.

Here's what was happening in your first 3 attempts:

  s.lower()  →  converts "hello" to "hello" (all lowercase)
  set("aEiOu")  →  contains {'a', 'E', 'i', 'O', 'u'}

So when you checked 'e' in {'a', 'E', 'i', 'O', 'u'}, it returned False
because lowercase 'e' is NOT the same character as uppercase 'E' in Python.

The rule: if you .lower() the input, your comparison values must ALSO be
all lowercase. You fixed this in attempt 4 by using vowels = "aeiou" —
all lowercase, matching the lowercased input. That's the right fix.

General principle: whenever you normalize case in one place (input), you
must normalize it in ALL places that compare against that input (lookup
values, sets, strings). Python treats 'e' and 'E' as completely different
characters.
